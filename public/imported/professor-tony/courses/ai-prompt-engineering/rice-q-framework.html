02

Deep Dive

R Role — Set the Expertise

Assigning a role activates relevant knowledge patterns and writing styles. The more specific the role, the more targeted the response.

Weak
"You are a fitness expert"
Strong
"You are a Doctor of Physical Therapy (DPT) with 15+ years of orthopedic rehabilitation experience, certified in dry needling and IASTM, who stays current with JOSPT guidelines"
  • Include years of experience or seniority level
  • Mention specific certifications (CSCS, RDN, DPT, etc.)
  • Reference relevant methodologies or evidence sources
  • Combine roles for hybrid expertise: "Sports dietitian with strength coaching background"

I Instructions — Define the Action

Clear instructions eliminate guesswork. Use action verbs and be explicit about deliverables.

Weak
"Help me with a patient education handout"
Strong
"Create a 1-page patient education handout for ACL post-op care. Include: 1) Week 1-2 exercises with sets/reps, 2) Ice/elevation protocol, 3) Red flags requiring immediate contact, 4) Follow-up appointment schedule"
  • Start with a strong action verb: create, design, analyze, develop
  • Specify quantity: "5 exercises," "3 meal options," "8-week program"
  • Break complex tasks into numbered components
  • State what NOT to include (equally important)

C Context — Provide Background

Context grounds the AI in your specific situation. Without it, you get generic responses.

Weak
"Write a meal plan for weight loss"
Strong
"Client: 28-year-old female CrossFit athlete (5x/week), desk job, 5'6" 155lbs, goal is body recomposition while supporting training. Prefers to limit dairy, budget $100-125/week, meal preps on Sundays."
  • Include patient/client demographics and relevant history
  • Mention constraints: equipment, time, budget, setting
  • Share goals and what success looks like
  • Provide industry-specific details that affect the output

E Examples — Show Don't Tell

Examples are the most powerful way to communicate style, tone, and format. One good example beats paragraphs of description.

Example Pattern
Phase 1 (Weeks 0-2) - Protection Phase:

GOALS:
• Control pain and swelling (VAS <3/10)
• Achieve full passive extension (0°)

EXERCISES:
1. Quad sets - 10 reps × 10 sec holds, 3x/day
Cue: "Tighten thigh, push knee flat into towel"
  • Provide 2-3 examples for pattern recognition
  • Include both positive examples (do this) and negative (not this)
  • Match examples to your exact use case
  • Show the transformation: input → desired output

Q Quality — Set Standards

Quality constraints shape the final output. Be specific about format, length, tone, and evaluation criteria.

Quality Constraints
Requirements:
• All exercises must include specific dosing (sets × reps × frequency)
• Cite relevant CPGs where applicable (e.g., MOON guidelines)
• Use standardized outcome measures (LEFS, NPRS)
• HEP should use 6th-grade reading level
• Include regression AND progression for each exercise
  • Reference clinical guidelines or evidence standards
  • Set reading level for patient materials (5th-6th grade)
  • Specify formatting requirements (bullet points, tables, headers)
  • Include a checklist the AI should verify against
