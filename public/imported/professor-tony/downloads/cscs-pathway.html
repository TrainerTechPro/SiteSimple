Eligibility Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree (or currently enrolled as a college senior)
  • Degree must be from an accredited institution
  • Degree can be in any field (Kinesiology preferred but not required)
  • Current CPR/AED certification
For Current Students: You can take the CSCS exam in your final semester before graduation. Your certification becomes active upon degree completion.

Section Questions Time Content Areas

12-Week Study Plan

Weeks 1-2
Exercise Science Foundation
Muscular, cardiovascular, respiratory, and nervous systems. Energy systems and bioenergetics.
Weeks 3-4
Biomechanics & Exercise Technique
Movement analysis, resistance training technique, plyometric technique.
Weeks 5-6
Program Design Fundamentals
Periodization, exercise selection, training variables, program progressions.
Weeks 7-8
Testing & Evaluation
Fitness assessments, performance testing, data interpretation, athlete monitoring.
Weeks 9-10
Nutrition & Sport Psychology
Macronutrients, hydration, supplements, motivation, goal setting.
Weeks 11-12
Review & Practice Exams
Full practice tests, weak area review, exam strategies.

Study Tips for Success

  1. Master the "Big 4" lifts - Know squat, bench, deadlift, and power clean technique inside and out
  2. Understand periodization - Linear, undulating, block—know when to apply each
  3. Know your energy systems - ATP-PC, glycolytic, oxidative—training applications for each
  4. Practice program design - Be able to design programs for various sports and goals
  5. Learn the testing protocols - 1RM testing, Wingate, VO2max, vertical jump, agility tests
  6. Take practice exams - Simulate test conditions and timing
Common Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Underestimating the breadth of the exam
  • Focusing only on the science, neglecting practical application
  • Not practicing with timed conditions
  • Skipping sport psychology and administration sections
